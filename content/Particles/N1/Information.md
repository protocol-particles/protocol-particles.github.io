Information is a fundamental Protocol Particle of [[Particles/N1/|Level -1]]
found in all complex systems. Information [[Dependence.md|requires]]
[[Energy.md|energy]] to be represented via some kind of encoding. Information is
generally imperfect since [[Time.md|time]] does not exist within the fundamental
particle barrier. Observability is therefore a critical concern for players and
environments in game theoretic terminology.

### Framing

Framing determines how information is presented and perceived which implies that
framing may be a strategy in more or less [[Energy.md#MEV|adversarial]]
environments.

### Oberservability

Observability provides the inputs that allow for interactions with any given
system. Due to the imperfect nature of information itself, it follows that
observability will also always be imperfect to the extend that anything that can
be observed may already be outdated by the [[Time.md|time]] of observation.
Humans for instance are generally only able to observe the world as it once has
been, which means that humans are future blind. Humans employ
[[Phenomena/Strategies/Transition/Computation/|computational]] strategies like
[[Simulation.md|simulation]] in order to aid that problem.

### Subjectivity

Subjectivity is a [[Phenomena/Gravitropy/Space/index.md#Proximity|localized]]
phenomenon expressing some kind of [[Phenomena/Gravitropy/|gravitropic]]
priority or utility function. Adjacent to the idea of the selfish gene[^1],
anthropologically[^2] speaking, subjectivity may be a critical component for
survival. Subjectivity can be turned against the subject itself, which indicates
the existence of [[Rotation.md|rotation]] or [[Inversion.md|inversion]]
strategies. Tragically, subjects often [[Weaponization.md|weaponize]] their own
subjectivity against themselves wherever the shades between
[[Balance.md#Thresholds|thresholds]] and [[Balance.md#Equilibria|equilibria]]
cannot be easily separated.

[^1]: [The Selfish Gene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gene-centered_view_of_evolution)

[^2]: [Anthropology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthropology)
