Space is a mechanical phenomenon enabling higher order range of
[[Behaviour.md|motion]] by creating [[Particles/P2/|distance]] between distinct
boundaries, without which space cannot exist. Said boundaries are defined by all
particles within the [[Particles/P0/|energetic]] and
[[Particles/P1/|fundamental]] barrier. The emerging quantities of proximity
within any given space open up [[Gravity.md#Tensions|tensions]] between players
and environments in game theoretic terminology. The default behaviour of is
[[Direction.md|dimensional]] occupation which depends on the
[[Information.md|observer's]] point of view.

### Absence

The [[Inversion.md|inversion]] of space is absence, which plays a key role in
the field of information theory[^1]. Information content may be increased by the
information that is missing or by an action that has not been done.

### Action Space

The action space of any given environment describes what is possible within its
own boundaires. [[Entropy.md|Entropy]] dictates any [[Behaviour.md|behaviour]]
that can happen will happen eventually, given a sufficient [[Energy.md|energy]]
[[Time.md|time]] relationship. It must be noted that action spaces are distinct
from [[Intention.md#Rule-Sets|rule sets]] because action spaces are fundamental,
whereas rule sets are artificial. What is not allowed may still be possible.

[^1]: [Information Theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_theory)
