Space is a mechanical phenomenon enabling higher order range of
[[Behaviour.md|motion]] by creating [[Particles/P2/|distance]] between distinct
boundary conditions, without which space cannot exist. Said boundary conditions
are defined by all particles within the [[Particles/P0/|energetic]] and
[[Particles/P1/|fundamental]] barrier. The emerging
[[#Quantum-Space|quantities]] of proximity within any given space open up
[[Balance.md#Equilibria|equilibria]] and [[Balance.md#Thresholds|thresholds]]
between players and environments in game theoretic terminology. The default
behaviour of space is the uninverted [[Direction.md|dimensional]] occupation
which depends on the [[Information.md|observer's]] point of view.

### Absence

The [[Inversion.md|inversion]] of space is absence, which plays a key role in
the field of information theory[^1]. Information content may be increased by the
[[Information.md|information]] that is missing or by an action that has not been
done. Information gain via information absence is by no means paradoxical once
we understand that different information particles bind to different boundary
conditions within the surrounding action space. By logical consequence, the
resulting relationships between those boundary conditions can then either be
[[Collaboration.md|collaborative]] or [[Competition.md|competetive]].

### Action Space

The action space of any given environment describes what is possible within its
own boundaires. [[Entropy.md|Entropy]] dictates any [[Behaviour.md|behaviour]]
that can happen will happen eventually, given a sufficient [[Energy.md|energy]]
[[Time.md|time]] relationship. It must be noted that action spaces are distinct
from [[Intention.md#Rule-Sets|rule sets]] because action spaces are fundamental,
whereas rule sets are artificial. What is not allowed may still be possible.

### Context

Context is the deliberate limitation of specific boundary conditions that allow
for the precise [[Position.md|reference]] of [[Information.md|information]].
Context is a critical component across all means of communication and signal
processing[^2].

### Quantum Space

Quantum space is simply speaking the [[#Action-Space|action space]] of
quantities. It may be thought of as an [[Abstraction.md|abstract]]
[[Abstraction.md#Concepts|concept]] allowing for the quantitative computation of
[[Mathematics.md|numbers]].
