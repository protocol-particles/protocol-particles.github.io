Gravity is the [[Information.md|structural]] accumulation of
[[Energy.md|energy]]. This phenomenon unfolds universally in any complex system.
It can be thought of as the [[Inversion.md|inversion]] of
[[Entropy.md|entropy]], because gravity causes any given thing to compress over
[[Time.md|time]] within the available [[Space.md#Action-Space|action space]].
The [[Direction.md|directionality]] of gravity may or may not be desirable for
either players or environments in game theoretic terms, because gravity gives
rise to second order effects, which may or may not be
[[Weaponization.md|weaponized]].

### Focal Points

Focal points[^1], are [[Position.md|positions]] of energy
[[Phenomena/Strategies/Transition/|transfer]]. Those positions of
[[Energy.md#Power|power]] may curve any given [[Space.md#Action-Space|action
space]] or [[Intention.md#Rule-Sets|rule set]]. For that reason its underlying
dynamics should be studied in more detail.

### Hardness

Hardness[^2] is the desirable form of [[Balance.md#Equilibria|equilibria]] that
exhibit relatively low levels of [[Entropy.md|entropy]], which is most relevant
in [[Competition.md#Markets|economic]] arenas. Financial assets may be
considered more valuable if they can prove exceptional levels of hardness in key
[[Direction.md|dimensions]] of their existence. Examples for those dimensions
may be in the order of reliable property rights and resistance against
devaluation over long periods of [[Time.md|time]].

### Tensions

Tensions are focal points providing ranges of desirable
[[Balance.md#Equilibria|equilibria]] reinforcing themselves. Tensions define
playful games to flourish in [[Symbiosis.md|symbiosis]].
