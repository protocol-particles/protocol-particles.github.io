Sports can be viewed through the lens of [[Phenomena/Gravitropy/|gravitropic
topolgies]] in the [[Phenomena/Gravitropy/Space/index.md#Context|context]] of
Brazilian Jiu Jitus[^1]. For the sake of this argument we are looking at the
[[Position.md|position]] of a rear naked choke[^2] combined with a body
triangle[^3]. Important to point out in this example is that we have to consider
two distinct, but interacting gravitropic topolgies of the same kind. That means
our [[Phenomena/Gravitropy/index.md#Dimensions|gravitropic dimensions]] of
interest and their respective correlations are equal. One of the two gravitropic
topologies unfolds along the shoulder line, and the other unfolds along the hip
line of the defender. The gravitropic dimensions in our example are described as
attention and execution. Attention defines the cognitive focus that each
grappler has on themselves and their respective contender. Execution does then
define the physical control that each grappler has about their own body, whether
they attack or defend. In this setup, cognitive attention, physical movement,
shoulder line and hip line do all represent
[[Phenomena/Gravitropy/Gravity/|gravitational]] centers.

> [!question] Questions
>
> > [!abstract]- Which role plays the concept of
> > [[Phenomena/Strategies/Transition/|transitions]] in Brazilian Jiu Jitsu?
> >
> > Most focus in BJJ is typically put on clearly defined positions. The state
> > transitions performed by the human body between those positions are complex
> > and by comparison lesser understood. Since almost nobody pays much attention
> > to this area, the sport of BJJ may greatly benefit from more detailed
> > analysis and practical examples. Further, studies in this area may help to
> > discover more general principles of transitions that are then more broadly
> > applicable to other competitive arenas as well.
>
> > [!abstract]- How does the [[Phenomena/Gravitropy/Gravity/|intensity]] of a
> > workout affect athletic performance?
> >
> > It is commonly known that consistency beats all other dimensions in physical
> > training. What is not so well established conceptually is how varying
> > structures of training schedules affect the athletic progress. Or said
> > differently, the ways in which we train, how often, under which circumstances
> > and in which intensity causes greatly different outcomes to be realized in
> > terms of athletic performance and physical recovery.
