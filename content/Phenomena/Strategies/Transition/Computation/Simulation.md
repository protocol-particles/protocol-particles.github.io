Simulation is the [[Emergence.md|creative]] process of constructing
hyperthetical [[Space.md|boundary]] conditions within a specific
[[Space.md#Context|context]]. In the world of humans, simulations appear to be
everywhere. The human brain is thought of as a simulation machine. Further, the
simulation hypothesis[^1] states that reality itself as perceived by humans may
not be more than a simulation either.

### Thought Experiments

Thought experiments[^2] describe the [[Intention.md|cognitive]] kind of
[[Phenomena/Strategies/|strategies]] that help humans to illuminate the
[[Information.md|uncertainties]] of the world around them. In that sense,
thought experiments resemble the same kind of outward energy
[[Direction.md|distribution]] on a cognitive level that [[Entropy.md|entropy]]
exercises [[Particles/P0/|energetically]].

### Mental Models

Mental models[^3] describe the [[Intention.md|cognitive]] kind of
[[Phenomena/Strategies/|strategies]] that help humans to manage the already
acquired [[Information.md|knowledge]] of the world around them. In that sense,
mental models resemble the same kind of inward energy
[[Direction.md|accumulation]] on a cognitive level that [[Gravity.md|gravity]]
exercises [[Particles/P0/|energetically]]. From there it follows that mental
models are the [[Inversion.md|inversion]] of [[#Thought-Experiments|thought
experiments]].
