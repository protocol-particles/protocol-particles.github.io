Abstraction generalizes [[Phenomena/Gravitropy/Space/index.md#Context|domain]]
specific [[Information.md|details]] in order to increase the utility function of
an [[Interface.md|interface]]. The resulting conceptualization of information
allows it to be universally understood by the majority of a population.

### Concepts

Concepts are [[Entropy.md#Permutations|permutations]] of
[[Dependence.md|connected]] [[Information.md|information]] that form
[[Phenomena/Gravitropy/Gravity/|gravitational]] centers due to their
[[Energy.md|energetically]] dense nature. Concepts can therefore only be found
in structurally [[Balance.md#Equilibria|convex]] pockets of any given
[[Phenomena/Strategies/Transition/Computation/|computational]]
[[Phenomena/Gravitropy/Space/|space]]. The amount of concepts we know[^1] about
is estimated to be in the order of $\frac{1}{10^{600}}$, which is in no small
part due to the fact that humans lack the linguistic[^2] capabilities to
describe most concepts.

[^1]: [the Amount of Concepts we know](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dkpDjd2nHgo&t=2447s)

[^2]: [Linguistic Relativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linguistic_relativity)
